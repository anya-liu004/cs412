About Benjamin Franklin

Benjamin Franklin (January 17, 1706 - April 17, 1790) was an American polymath: a writer, scientist, inventor, statesman, diplomat, printer, publisher and political philosopher.

Among the most influential intellectuals of his time, Franklin was one of the Founding Fathers of the United States, drafting and signing the Declaration of Independence.
